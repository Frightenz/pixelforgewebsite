PixelForge policy

Privacy Policy

PixelForge is designed to keep image generation work under your control. This policy explains what the app collects, what stays local, and how to contact support.

Effective date: July 2, 2026

Summary Data collected Local app data Third parties Retention and deletion Consent controls Contact

Summary

PixelForge does not collect personal data through the app and does not track you across apps or websites. The app privacy manifest declares no collected data types, no tracking, and no tracking domains.

If you choose to email support, the information you send is used only to respond to your request.

Data collected by PixelForge

PixelForge does not collect, sell, rent, or share personal data from the app for advertising, analytics, profiling, or tracking.

The app may access device capabilities only when needed for app functionality, such as checking available storage, reading file timestamps for local files, saving an image you choose to save, or using device authentication for privacy lock features.

Local app data

Prompts, generated images, imported source images, model files, gallery records, app settings, and prompt recipes are stored locally on your device unless you choose to export, save, or share them.

Exports and shared files are controlled by the destination you choose, such as Photos, Files, AirDrop, Messages, Mail, or another app.

Third parties

PixelForge does not include third-party advertising or analytics SDKs. Apple may process App Store purchases, subscriptions, refunds, downloads, crash diagnostics, and platform services under Apple's own policies.

PixelForge does not receive your payment card details. Apple may provide transaction status needed to unlock purchased features.

Retention and deletion

Local app data remains on your device until you delete it in the app, remove exported files from their destinations, or delete PixelForge from your device.

Support emails are retained only as long as reasonably needed to respond, troubleshoot, keep business records, or comply with legal obligations. You can request deletion of support correspondence by emailing support.

Consent controls

You can control app permissions in iOS or iPadOS Settings. You can revoke Photos access, stop using export or share features, disable privacy lock if enabled, or delete the app and its local data from your device.

If a future PixelForge release changes data handling, the policy and App Store privacy information will be updated before or with that release.

Children

PixelForge is not directed to children and does not knowingly collect personal data from children.

Contact

For privacy questions, support, or deletion requests related to support correspondence, use the PixelForge support page.
